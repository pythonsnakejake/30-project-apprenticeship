** CORRECTIONS **
The .split() Behavior: When you run ",".split() on "  ord773 ,  5.25 , zone_c  ", it splits the string exactly at the commas. It leaves you with a list of three separate strings: ["  ord773 ", "  5.25 ", " zone_c  "].

The Bottleneck: Once a string is split into a list, you can no longer call string methods like .strip() or .upper() on the list object directly—you have to apply them to each individual element inside the list!

The Target Order: Instead of trying to use .replace(), your process should look like this:

Identify & Split: Split by the delimiter (// or ,).

Clean Elements: Loop through the split elements, using .strip() to clear whitespaces and .upper() to normalize the text casing.
